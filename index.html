Isaac Asimov

One of science fiction's biggest figures

His life and work

Isaac Asimov's contributions to the science fiction genre come through his novels and short stories. He believed that the "Three Laws of Robotics", a set of rules he devised that form an organizing principle for all of his robotic-based-fiction, would become his more enduring work alongside the "Foundation" series.

A brief timeline of his life:

  • 1920 Born in Petrovichi, Russia
  • 1923 Travelled to America with his family
  • 1928 Became a naturalized U.S. citizen
  • 1939 Finished his Bachelor of Science degree at University Extension
  • 1937 Began writing his first science fiction story "Cosmic Corkscrew"
  • 1938 Sold the third story he finished to Amazing Stories
  • 1941 Completed his Master of Arts degree in chemistry
  • 1942 Published the first of his Foundation stories
  • 1948 Earned a Doctor of Philsophy degree in chemistry
  • 1949 Joined the Bostonn University School of Medicine teaching biochemistry
  • 1972 Published his novel "The Gods Themselves" which won several awards and received general acclaim
  • 1977 Suffered a heart attack which years later forced him to undergo a triple bypass surgery
  • 1983 Contracted HIV from a blood transfusion during the surgery
  • 1992 Passed away in Manhattan

The Three Laws of Robotics

  • First Law: A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.
  • Second Law: A robot must obey the orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.
  • Third Law: A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.
